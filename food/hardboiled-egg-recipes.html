⭐ Start Here: Perfect Hard-Boiled Eggs

  1. Bring a pot of water to a rolling boil (enough to cover the eggs by an inch).
  2. Gently lower in cold eggs with a spoon. Boil 11–12 minutes for fully set yolks (9–10 for slightly creamy).
  3. Immediately transfer to an ice-water bath for 5+ minutes. This stops cooking and makes peeling easy.
  4. Tap, roll, and peel under running water. Older eggs (7–10 days) peel more easily than fresh.
Classic Dishes

Deviled Eggs

The crowd-pleaser. Creamy seasoned yolk piped back into the whites.

⏱ 15 minMakes 12 halvesParty favorite

Ingredients

  • 6 hard-boiled eggs
  • 3 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 tsp Dijon or yellow mustard
  • 1 tsp white vinegar (or pickle juice)
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • Paprika, for dusting
  • Optional: chopped chives, hot sauce

Method

  1. Halve eggs lengthwise. Pop yolks into a bowl; set whites on a plate.
  2. Mash yolks with mayo, mustard, vinegar, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  3. Spoon (or pipe with a zip-top bag, corner snipped) the filling back into the whites.
  4. Dust with paprika and top with chives. Chill until serving.
Tip: For ultra-smooth filling, push the yolks through a fine sieve before mixing.

Egg Salad

Five minutes from peeled eggs to a sandwich filling.

⏱ 10 minServes 2–3Lunch

Ingredients

  • 4 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
  • 3 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 stalk celery, finely diced
  • 1 tbsp minced red onion or chives
  • Salt & pepper
  • Bread, lettuce, or crackers to serve

Method

  1. Chop eggs into rough ½-inch pieces.
  2. Stir together with mayo, mustard, celery, and onion.
  3. Season with salt and pepper; taste and adjust.
  4. Serve on toasted bread with lettuce, or scoop onto crackers.
Tip: A squeeze of lemon or a pinch of dill brightens it up.

Pickled Eggs

Tangy make-ahead snack. Beet juice turns them magenta.

⏱ 15 min + 2 daysMakes 6Make ahead

Ingredients

  • 6 peeled hard-boiled eggs
  • 1 cup white or cider vinegar
  • ½ cup water
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ sliced onion, 1 tsp peppercorns
  • Optional: juice from a can of beets (for color)

Method

  1. Simmer vinegar, water, sugar, salt, and spices for 2 minutes; cool slightly.
  2. Pack eggs and onion into a clean jar.
  3. Pour the warm brine over to fully submerge the eggs.
  4. Seal, refrigerate at least 48 hours (best after a week). Keeps ~1 month.
Safety: Always store pickled eggs in the fridge, never at room temperature.

Ramen / Noodle Bowls

A halved egg on top of any brothy bowl. Marinate for extra flavor.

⏱ 5 min (+marinate)Topping

Quick marinade (optional)

  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp water
  • 1 tbsp mirin or sugar
  • Peeled eggs to soak

Method

  1. For plain: simply halve and lay on top of the finished bowl.
  2. For ramen eggs: soak peeled eggs in the marinade 2–12 hours in the fridge.
  3. Halve and place cut-side up so the yolk shows.
Tip: Slightly under-cooked eggs (9 min) give a jammier center that's ideal here.

Scotch Eggs

Egg wrapped in sausage, breaded, and fried. A bit of a project, very worth it.

⏱ 40 minMakes 4Advanced

Ingredients

  • 4 hard-boiled eggs, peeled
  • 1 lb (450g) sausage meat
  • 1 raw egg, beaten
  • ½ cup flour
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs (panko)
  • Oil for frying

Method

  1. Divide sausage into 4; flatten each into a patty.
  2. Wrap each boiled egg fully in sausage, sealing the seams.
  3. Roll in flour, then beaten egg, then breadcrumbs.
  4. Deep-fry at 325°F (165°C) for 7–8 min until golden, or bake at 400°F (200°C) for 25 min.
  5. Rest a few minutes, then halve and serve.
Tip: Frying then finishing in the oven ensures the sausage cooks through without burning the crust.

Egg Curry

Halved eggs simmered in a spiced tomato-onion gravy. Serve with rice.

⏱ 30 minServes 3–4Dinner

Ingredients

  • 6 hard-boiled eggs, peeled
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves + 1 tbsp ginger
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tsp each: cumin, coriander, turmeric
  • 1 tsp garam masala, chili to taste
  • Oil, salt, fresh cilantro

Method

  1. Optional: pan-fry whole eggs in a little oil until golden; set aside.
  2. Sauté onions in oil until deep golden, then add garlic and ginger.
  3. Stir in the ground spices, then the tomato puree. Cook until oil separates.
  4. Add ½ cup water for a gravy; simmer 5 minutes.
  5. Halve the eggs, nestle them in, simmer 5 more minutes. Finish with garam masala and cilantro.
Tip: Scoring the eggs lightly before frying helps the gravy cling.

Eggs in Fried Rice / Hash

Chop and stir into fried rice or a crispy potato hash at the end.

⏱ 15 minServes 2Dinner

Ingredients

  • 2–3 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
  • 2 cups cold cooked rice (or diced cooked potato)
  • 1 cup mixed veg (peas, carrot, scallion)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oil, salt & pepper

Method

  1. Heat oil in a wide pan over high heat.
  2. Stir-fry the veg 2–3 minutes, then add rice (or potato) and press to crisp.
  3. Add soy sauce and toss until heated through.
  4. Fold in the chopped egg at the very end so it warms but stays intact.
Tip: Day-old cold rice fries up far better than fresh, which turns mushy.

Condiments & Pairings

Green Mango Chutney

Tangy-sweet, lightly spiced condiment from unripe mangoes — perfect with curried egg filling, egg curry, or spread on an egg sandwich.

⏱ 45 minMakes ~2 cupsMake aheadVegan

Ingredients

  • 2 large green (unripe) mangoes, peeled & diced (~3 cups)
  • ¾ cup sugar (or jaggery)
  • ½ cup white or cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • ½ tsp chili flakes (to taste)
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ¼ tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp salt
  • Optional: ¼ cup raisins, ½ tsp nigella or mustard seeds

Method

  1. If using mustard/nigella seeds, sizzle them in 1 tbsp oil for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2. Add ginger and garlic; stir 1 minute, then add the diced green mango.
  3. Stir in sugar, vinegar, chili, cumin, turmeric, salt, and raisins.
  4. Simmer uncovered over low-medium heat, stirring often, 30–35 minutes until the mango softens and the mixture is jammy and glossy.
  5. Cool completely — it thickens further as it cools. Spoon into a clean jar.
Storage: Keeps in a sealed jar in the fridge for up to 3–4 weeks. The flavor deepens after a day or two.

Thai Mango Chicken Soup

A bright, lightly spicy coconut broth with tender chicken and sweet mango — finished with lime and Thai basil.

⏱ 35 minServes 4Dinner

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) boneless chicken thighs, sliced
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp red Thai curry paste
  • 1 ripe mango, diced (plus ½ pureed)
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger, 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce, 1 tsp brown sugar
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • Juice of 1 lime, Thai basil & cilantro
  • 1 red chili, sliced (optional)

Method

  1. Sauté ginger, garlic, and curry paste in a little oil for 1 minute until fragrant.
  2. Add chicken and brown lightly on all sides.
  3. Pour in broth, coconut milk, and the mango puree. Stir in fish sauce and sugar.
  4. Simmer 15 minutes until the chicken is cooked through.
  5. Add bell pepper and diced mango; cook 3–4 more minutes.
  6. Off heat, stir in lime juice. Top with Thai basil, cilantro, and chili.
Tip: Pureeing half the mango sweetens and thickens the broth; the diced half keeps fresh texture.

Mango Coconut Curry Soup

A creamy, blended vegan soup — sweet mango and coconut balanced by warm curry spices. Serve hot or chilled.

⏱ 30 minServes 4VeganBlended

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe mangoes, diced (~2½ cups)
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 1 tbsp yellow or red curry powder/paste
  • ½ tsp turmeric, pinch cayenne
  • 1 tbsp lime juice, salt to taste
  • Cilantro & toasted coconut, to garnish

Method

  1. Sauté onion in oil until soft, then add garlic, ginger, curry, turmeric, and cayenne; cook 1 minute.
  2. Add mango and broth; simmer 12–15 minutes until the mango is very soft.
  3. Stir in coconut milk and warm through.
  4. Blend until smooth (immersion or stand blender). Add a splash of broth if too thick.
  5. Season with lime and salt. Serve hot, or chill for a refreshing cold soup.
  6. Garnish with cilantro and toasted coconut.
Tip: Blend carefully while hot — vent the lid and start on low to avoid splatter.
