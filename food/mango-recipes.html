Mango Sauces

Mango Chutney Easy

Prep: 15 min
Cook: 20 min
Yield: 2 cups

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe mangoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon mustard seeds (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare base: Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and listen for them to pop (about 30 seconds).
  2. Sauté aromatics: Add chopped red onion, garlic, and ginger. Sauté for 3-4 minutes until fragrant and onion begins to soften.
  3. Add mangoes: Stir in diced mangoes and cook for 3 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add liquids and spices: Pour in apple cider vinegar and add brown sugar, cayenne pepper, and turmeric. Stir well to combine.
  5. Simmer: Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 12-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until mixture thickens and mango breaks down slightly.
  6. Season: Taste and adjust with salt, pepper, or more sugar for balance.
  7. Cool: Transfer to a jar and cool completely. Store in refrigerator for up to 3 weeks.
💡 Tip: Serve with grilled meats, on cheese boards, or with Indian dishes. For a smoother consistency, blend partially or completely with an immersion blender.

Mango BBQ Sauce Easy

Prep: 10 min
Cook: 15 min
Yield: 1.5 cups

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mango puree (fresh or frozen)
  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine wet ingredients: In a medium saucepan, whisk together mango puree, ketchup, apple cider vinegar, honey, Worcestershire sauce, and soy sauce.
  2. Add aromatics and spices: Stir in minced garlic, smoked paprika, cumin, and cayenne pepper. Mix until well combined.
  3. Heat: Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally.
  4. Simmer: Continue simmering for 12-15 minutes to allow flavors to blend and sauce to thicken slightly.
  5. Season: Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or more cayenne for heat.
  6. Cool and store: Transfer to a jar and cool. Use immediately or refrigerate for up to 2 weeks.
💡 Tip: Brush on grilled chicken, ribs, or shrimp during the last 3-5 minutes of cooking. Also excellent as a dipping sauce for spring rolls.

Spicy Mango Habanero Sauce Medium

Prep: 15 min
Cook: 25 min
Yield: 1.5 cups

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe mangoes, peeled and diced
  • 2-3 habanero peppers, seeds removed (for less heat)
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lime juice
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cloves

Instructions:

  1. Prepare peppers: Carefully remove seeds and membranes from habaneros (wear gloves!). This reduces heat while keeping flavor.
  2. Combine ingredients: In a saucepan, add mango pieces, habanero peppers, red bell pepper, garlic, lime juice, and rice vinegar.
  3. Cook: Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce to medium-low and simmer for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Blend: Use an immersion blender to reach desired consistency (chunky or smooth). For smoother sauce, transfer to food processor.
  5. Season: Add honey, salt, black pepper, and cloves. Stir and taste, adjusting heat or sweetness as needed.
  6. Final simmer: Simmer for another 2-3 minutes if needed to reach desired thickness.
  7. Cool and store: Cool completely and transfer to jars. Refrigerate for up to 3 weeks.
💡 Tip: Perfect for tacos, grilled fish, or as a spicy condiment. Add less habanero for milder sauce. For extra heat, keep seeds in.

Sweet Mango Reduction Easy

Prep: 10 min
Cook: 20 min
Yield: 1 cup

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups mango puree
  • 1/4 cup honey or agave
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 vanilla bean, split (or 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract)
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Combine: Pour mango puree into a heavy-bottomed saucepan. Add honey and lime juice.
  2. Add vanilla: If using vanilla bean, scrape seeds into mixture and add the pod. If using extract, set aside for later.
  3. Reduce: Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce to medium. Simmer uncovered for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Test consistency: Sauce should coat the back of a spoon. It will thicken more as it cools.
  5. Add vanilla extract: If using extract instead of bean, stir in now. Add a pinch of salt.
  6. Cool: Remove from heat and let cool completely in the pan. Transfer to jars.
💡 Tip: Drizzle over vanilla ice cream, desserts, cheesecake, or use as a glaze for roasted duck. Can be made ahead and frozen for up to 3 months.

Spicy Mango Lime Sauce Easy

Prep: 10 min
Cook: 0 min (No cook)
Yield: 1.5 cups

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mango puree or finely diced mango
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1-2 jalapeños, minced (seeds removed for less heat)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Combine mango and lime: In a bowl, mix mango puree with lime juice.
  2. Add herbs and spices: Stir in cilantro, minced jalapeños, minced garlic, cumin, and red pepper flakes.
  3. Season: Add salt, pepper, and honey if desired for sweetness balance.
  4. Chill: Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving to let flavors meld.
  5. Taste and adjust: Before serving, taste and adjust lime, heat, or salt as needed.
💡 Tip: Perfect fresh sauce for fish tacos, grilled fish, or quesadillas. Best served fresh within 2-3 days. Can be made up to 24 hours ahead.

Mango Salad Dressings

Mango Lime Vinaigrette Easy

Prep: 10 min
Yield: 1 cup

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mango puree or finely diced mango
  • 1/4 cup lime juice (about 3 limes)
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 shallot, minced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Combine acids: In a small bowl or jar, whisk together lime juice and rice vinegar.
  2. Add aromatics: Stir in minced shallot, garlic, Dijon mustard, and honey.
  3. Incorporate mango: Add mango puree and whisk until well combined.
  4. Emulsify: Slowly drizzle in olive oil while whisking constantly to create an emulsion.
  5. Season: Taste and adjust with salt, pepper, or red pepper flakes.
  6. Store: Transfer to a jar. Keep refrigerated for up to 5 days. Shake before each use.
💡 Tip: Perfect for spinach, arugula, or mixed green salads. Also works on grilled fish or chicken. Use with greens with mild flavors to let mango shine.

Creamy Mango Dressing Easy

Prep: 10 min
Yield: 1.5 cups

Ingredients:

  • 3/4 cup mango puree
  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt or sour cream
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and white pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Combine base: In a bowl, whisk together Greek yogurt (or sour cream) and mayonnaise until smooth.
  2. Add mango: Fold in mango puree gently until fully incorporated.
  3. Season: Add lime juice, honey, cumin, and paprika. Whisk until smooth.
  4. Adjust consistency: If too thick, thin with a tablespoon of lime juice or water at a time. If too thin, add more yogurt.
  5. Final flavor: Taste and adjust with salt, white pepper, or more lime juice. Fold in cilantro.
  6. Chill: Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving.
💡 Tip: Excellent on spinach salads, grilled chicken salads, or as a dip for fresh vegetables. Can be made 2-3 days ahead. Keeps refrigerated up to a week.

Spicy Mango Ginger Dressing Easy

Prep: 10 min
Yield: 1 cup

Ingredients:

  • 3/4 cup mango puree
  • 3 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon sriracha or chili paste
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Mix base: In a small bowl, whisk together sesame oil, rice vinegar, soy sauce, and lime juice.
  2. Add aromatics: Stir in grated ginger, minced garlic, and sriracha or chili paste.
  3. Incorporate mango: Add mango puree and whisk until well combined.
  4. Season: Add white pepper and taste for salt. Adjust heat level if needed.
  5. Store: Transfer to a jar and refrigerate. Shake well before each use.
💡 Tip: Fantastic on Asian-inspired salads with napa cabbage, edamame, and sesame seeds. Also excellent on shrimp or chicken salads. Keeps refrigerated for up to a week.

Honey Mango Mustard Dressing Easy

Prep: 8 min
Yield: 1 cup

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup mango puree
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons whole grain mustard
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine mustards: In a small bowl, mix Dijon mustard and whole grain mustard together.
  2. Add wet ingredients: Whisk in apple cider vinegar, honey, and mango puree.
  3. Emulsify: Slowly drizzle in olive oil while whisking constantly.
  4. Add spices: Stir in turmeric and cayenne pepper.
  5. Season: Taste and adjust with salt and pepper as needed.
  6. Store: Transfer to a jar and refrigerate. Shake before each use.
💡 Tip: Perfect for bitter greens like endive or radicchio. Also excellent as a dipping sauce for vegetables or as a sauce for grilled chicken or pork. Keeps refrigerated for up to 2 weeks.

Mango Chicken Dishes

Mango Marinated Grilled Chicken Medium

Prep: 15 min + 2-8 hours marinating
Cook: 15-20 min
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (or 8 thighs)
  • For Marinade:
  • 1 cup mango puree
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 jalapeño, minced (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare marinade: In a bowl, whisk together mango puree, olive oil, lime juice, and soy sauce.
  2. Add aromatics and spices: Stir in garlic, ginger, jalapeño (if using), cumin, turmeric, salt, and pepper.
  3. Marinate chicken: Place chicken in a zip-top bag or shallow dish. Pour marinade over chicken, ensuring all pieces are coated.
  4. Refrigerate: Seal or cover and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, preferably 4-8 hours. Flip chicken halfway through if possible.
  5. Prepare grill: Preheat grill to medium-high heat. Oil grates to prevent sticking.
  6. Remove from marinade: Remove chicken from marinade, shaking off excess. Reserve about 1/4 cup marinade for basting.
  7. Grill: Place chicken on grill and cook for 6-7 minutes per side (for breasts) or 8-10 minutes per side (for thighs) depending on thickness. Chicken is done when internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  8. Baste: During last 2-3 minutes of cooking, brush reserved marinade on chicken.
  9. Rest: Remove from grill and let rest 5 minutes before serving.
💡 Tip: Serve with grilled pineapple, cilantro lime rice, or fresh mango salsa. Don't skip the resting period—it keeps chicken juicy. For indoor cooking, use a grill pan or skillet over medium-high heat.

Mango Glazed Chicken Breast Easy

Prep: 10 min
Cook: 20 min
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For Glaze:
  • 1 cup mango puree
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme (or 1/2 teaspoon dried)
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes

Instructions:

  1. Prepare glaze: In a small saucepan, combine mango puree, honey, balsamic vinegar, and Dijon mustard. Heat over medium heat until it simmers.
  2. Add aromatics: Stir in minced garlic, thyme, and red pepper flakes. Continue simmering for 3-5 minutes until slightly thickened. Set aside.
  3. Season chicken: Pat chicken breasts dry with paper towels. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
  4. Sear chicken: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add chicken and sear for 5 minutes per side until golden. Chicken won't be cooked through yet.
  5. Apply glaze: Reduce heat to medium. Spoon glaze over chicken, coating all pieces.
  6. Cook covered: Cover skillet with a lid and cook for 5-8 minutes until chicken reaches internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).
  7. Finish: If desired, brush with additional glaze and cook uncovered for 1 minute to caramelize.
  8. Rest and serve: Let rest 3 minutes. Spoon remaining glaze from pan over chicken before serving.
💡 Tip: Serve over jasmine rice or couscous. For extra glaze, double the recipe. Perfect for a weeknight dinner. Pairs well with steamed broccoli or roasted vegetables.

Mango Chicken Stir-Fry Medium

Prep: 20 min
Cook: 15 min
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs boneless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 cups diced mango (about 2 ripe mangoes)
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 2 cups fresh pineapple chunks
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • For Sauce:
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch + 2 tablespoons water (slurry)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, oyster sauce, lime juice, sesame oil, and red pepper flakes. In another small bowl, mix cornstarch with water to create a slurry. Set both aside.
  2. Heat wok or large skillet: Heat 1.5 tablespoons vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat until shimmering.
  3. Cook chicken: Add chicken pieces in a single layer (work in batches if needed). Let cook undisturbed for 2 minutes to develop color. Stir and cook for 3-4 more minutes until cooked through (internal temperature 165°F). Remove to a plate.
  4. Stir-fry vegetables: Add remaining oil to wok. Add garlic and ginger, stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add harder vegetables: Add bell peppers and red onion. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until softened but still crisp.
  6. Add mango and pineapple: Add mango chunks and pineapple. Toss gently and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  7. Return chicken: Add cooked chicken back to wok.
  8. Add sauce: Pour in sauce mixture and stir well. Add cornstarch slurry a little at a time, stirring, until sauce reaches desired thickness.
  9. Finish: Cook for 1-2 minutes more until sauce coats everything. Taste and adjust seasonings.
  10. Serve: Serve immediately over steamed rice or noodles. Garnish with fresh cilantro if desired.
💡 Tip: Don't overcook the mango—add it near the end so it stays fresh-tasting. Prep all ingredients before you start cooking since stir-fry is fast. Works great with cashews for added texture.

Tropical Mango Chicken (Baked) Easy

Prep: 15 min
Cook: 30 min
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs or drumsticks (bone-in, skin-on)
  • 1 cup mango chunks
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1/2 red onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Season chicken: Season chicken with salt and pepper.
  3. Prepare sauce: In a bowl, whisk together coconut milk, curry powder, turmeric, cayenne pepper, lime juice, and honey. Stir until spices are dissolved.
  4. Sauté aromatics: Heat 1 tablespoon oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and ginger, cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Sear chicken: Add chicken pieces and sear for 2-3 minutes per side until golden. Don't worry about cooking through.
  6. Add vegetables: Add red onion, bell peppers to the skillet around the chicken.
  7. Add sauce and mango: Pour coconut sauce over everything. Scatter mango chunks around the skillet.
  8. Bake: Cover with foil and bake for 20-25 minutes until chicken is cooked through (internal temperature 165°F) and vegetables are tender.
  9. Finish: Remove foil, taste sauce and adjust seasonings. Sprinkle with fresh cilantro.
  10. Serve: Serve over jasmine or basmati rice, scooping sauce over everything.
💡 Tip: This is a one-pan meal that's perfect for entertaining. Can be made ahead and reheated. For less heat, reduce cayenne. Unsweetened coconut milk works best for authentic flavor.

Mango Shrimp Dishes

Mango Lime Grilled Shrimp Medium

Prep: 20 min + 1-2 hours marinating
Cook: 5-6 min
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs large shrimp (21/25 count), peeled and deveined
  • For Marinade:
  • 3/4 cup mango puree
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Juice of 3 limes
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Pinch of cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Make marinade: In a bowl, whisk together mango puree, olive oil, and lime juice.
  2. Add aromatics and spices: Stir in garlic, cilantro, cumin, paprika, cayenne, salt, and pepper.
  3. Marinate shrimp: Add shrimp to a zip-top bag or shallow dish. Pour marinade over, ensuring all shrimp are coated.
  4. Refrigerate: Seal and marinate in refrigerator for 1-2 hours. Don't marinate longer—the acid can make shrimp mushy.
  5. Prepare grill: Preheat grill to medium-high heat. If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes first.
  6. Thread shrimp: Thread shrimp onto skewers (2 skewers per shrimp is best to prevent spinning).
  7. Remove from marinade: Remove skewers, letting excess marinade drip off.
  8. Grill: Place skewers on grill. Cook for 2-3 minutes per side until shrimp are pink and opaque (don't overcook).
  9. Rest briefly: Let rest 2 minutes before serving.
💡 Tip: Serve with lime wedges, cilantro, and grilled lime halves. Pair with cilantro lime rice or fresh salad. For pan-searing, use a very hot skillet for 2-3 minutes per side.

Mango Shrimp Tacos Medium

Prep: 20 min
Cook: 10 min
Serves: 4 (8-10 tacos)

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • For Shrimp Seasoning:
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • For Assembly:
  • Corn or flour tortillas (8-10)
  • 2 cups diced fresh mango
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro
  • Lime wedges
  • Your favorite hot sauce (optional)
  • Crema or sour cream (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare seasoning: In a small bowl, combine chili powder, cumin, paprika, garlic powder, cayenne, salt, and pepper.
  2. Season shrimp: Pat shrimp dry. Toss with seasoning mixture until evenly coated.
  3. Cook shrimp: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add shrimp in a single layer and cook 2-3 minutes per side until pink and opaque. Don't overcrowd the pan—work in batches if needed.
  4. Warm tortillas: Place tortillas directly over a gas flame for 15-20 seconds per side to char slightly, or warm in a skillet or microwave.
  5. Assemble tacos: Place a few shrimp on each tortilla. Top with diced mango, sliced red onion, and fresh cilantro.
  6. Serve: Serve with lime wedges, hot sauce, and crema on the side. Squeeze lime juice and drizzle sauce just before eating.
💡 Tip: Make a simple mango salsa by combining diced mango, red onion, cilantro, jalapeño, and lime juice—even better than plain mango. Set up a taco bar so guests can customize. Can prep shrimp seasoning and mango salsa ahead.

Spicy Mango Shrimp Skewers Medium

Prep: 15 min + 30 min marinating
Cook: 10-12 min
Serves: 4 (8 skewers)

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 mangoes, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into 1-inch chunks
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into 1-inch chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • For Marinade/Glaze:
  • 1/2 cup mango puree
  • 3 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 tablespoons sriracha or hot sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Make marinade: Whisk together mango puree, lime juice, soy sauce, honey, sriracha, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  2. Marinate shrimp: Place shrimp in a zip-top bag or shallow dish. Add half of the marinade, seal, and marinate in refrigerator for 30 minutes. Reserve other half for basting.
  3. Prepare skewers: Soak wooden skewers in water for 30 minutes. Thread shrimp and vegetables onto skewers alternating: shrimp, mango, bell pepper, onion, repeat.
  4. Preheat grill: Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  5. Grill skewers: Place skewers on grill. Cook for 3-4 minutes per side.
  6. Baste: During last minute of cooking, brush with reserved marinade.
  7. Finish: Remove from grill and let rest 2-3 minutes. Taste and adjust heat level.
💡 Tip: The mango and peppers cook at the same rate as the quick-cooking shrimp, making this a perfectly balanced dish. If you prefer less heat, use less sriracha. Serve over jasmine rice or with a cooling cucumber salad.

Mango Coconut Shrimp Medium

Prep: 15 min
Cook: 15 min
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • For Sauce:
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • 3/4 cup mango puree
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1-2 Thai chiles or 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • For Cooking:
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 4 green onions, sliced into 2-inch pieces
  • Fresh basil or cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare sauce: In a medium bowl, combine coconut milk, mango puree, fish sauce, lime juice, and honey. Stir until smooth. Set aside.
  2. Prepare aromatics: Mince garlic and ginger together. Slice Thai chiles (or use red pepper flakes).
  3. Cook aromatics: Heat oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add garlic, ginger, and chiles. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Cook shrimp: Add shrimp to the skillet, stirring to coat with oil. Cook for 2-3 minutes until starting to turn pink. Don't fully cook yet.
  5. Add vegetables: Add red and green bell peppers. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until peppers begin to soften.
  6. Add sauce: Pour coconut mango sauce over shrimp and peppers. Stir gently.
  7. Simmer: Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 3-4 minutes until shrimp are fully cooked (pink and opaque) and sauce is heated through. Stir occasionally.
  8. Add green onions: Stir in green onions.
  9. Garnish and serve: Taste and adjust seasonings. Serve over jasmine rice or rice noodles. Garnish with fresh basil or cilantro.
💡 Tip: This is a Thai-inspired curry. Don't skip fish sauce—it adds depth even though you won't taste fishiness. For a non-dairy version, use full-fat coconut milk. Can be made ahead and reheated gently. Add more mango for a sweeter curry.

General Tips for Cooking with Mango

Selection & Storage

  • 🥭 Choosing: Look for mangoes that yield slightly to pressure but aren't mushy. Color varies by variety—not all ripe mangoes are bright colored.
  • 🥭 Ripening: Place firm mangoes in a paper bag at room temperature for 3-5 days to ripen. Store ripe mangoes in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
  • 🥭 Puree: Blend peeled mango chunks with a little water to create puree. Freeze in ice cube trays for convenient portions.

Cutting Techniques

  • 🥭 Cheeks Method: Stand mango upright. Using a sharp knife, cut along the flat pit on both sides to remove the two "cheeks." Score the flesh in a crosshatch pattern without piercing the skin. Push the skin up from underneath to expose cubes.
  • 🥭 Peel Method: Peel the mango with a vegetable peeler, then slice flesh from the pit.
  • 🥭 Cube Method: After removing cheeks, use a sharp knife to cut into cubes, then slice away from skin with a spoon.

Flavor Combinations

  • 🥭 Classic: Mango + Lime + Cilantro + Garlic
  • 🥭 Warm Spices: Mango + Ginger + Turmeric + Cumin
  • 🥭 Heat: Mango + Habanero/Jalapeño + Lime + Garlic
  • 🥭 Creamy: Mango + Coconut + Vanilla + Cinnamon
  • 🥭 Asian: Mango + Soy + Ginger + Sesame Oil + Chiles

Cooking Notes

  • 🥭 Heat Sensitivity: Add diced or cubed mango near the end of cooking to preserve its fresh flavor and texture. It becomes mushy if overcooked.
  • 🥭 Puree Sauces: Mango puree works better in cooked sauces as it maintains body and absorbs other flavors.
  • 🥭 Marinating: Don't marinate seafood in mango puree for more than 2 hours—the acid can affect texture. 1-2 hours is ideal.
  • 🥭 Sourness Balance: Mango is naturally sweet; balance with lime juice or vinegar for brightness and prevent a one-note sweetness.
