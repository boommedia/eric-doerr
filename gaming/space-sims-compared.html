🚀 Space Sims Compared — X4 vs Elite vs Star Citizen

How X4: Foundations stacks up against Elite Dangerous & Star Citizen — plus how-to-use & binding guides for all three on your Thrustmaster T.16000M gear (HOTAS + Space Sim Duo).

⚡ The 30-second summary

X4: Foundations Empire

A 4X empire-builder wearing a space-sim's cockpit.
  • Start a lone pilot → end a CEO/admiral running factories & fleets
  • Own hundreds of ships, build stations, command from a map
  • Single-player, offline, heavily moddable
  • Deepest simulated economy of the three
Pick if: you want to build & command, not just fly. Finished & solo.

Elite Dangerous Pilot

A lone pilot in a 1:1 Milky Way.
  • Best-feeling flight model of the three
  • 400 billion star systems, real scale & exploration
  • Online (solo / private / open), released & stable
  • You are basically one person, one ship
Pick if: flying itself is the point and you love vastness.

Star Citizen Immersion

Maximum fidelity — walk everywhere, seamless planets.
  • Get up, walk your ship & stations, land on planets seamlessly
  • Highest graphical fidelity & immersion
  • Always-online MMO, perpetual alpha
  • Buggier & unfinished — but nothing else feels like it
Pick if: immersion > polish and you don't mind jank.

🧭 Where X4 is unique: Elite and Star Citizen are both about being a pilot. X4 lets you become an empire — you'll eventually spend as much time in the management map (assigning captains, planning factories, moving fleets) as in the cockpit. If you loved Elite's flying but wished you could build and command, or came from Star Citizen wanting that in a finished, single-player, moddable package, X4 is the natural pick.

Want to build?

Want to live in it?

🎮 Your hardware

T.16000M FCS HOTAS stick + throttle

One T.16000M stick + TWCS throttle.
  • Stick: Hall-effect (no drift), 16 buttons, 8-way hat, X/Y + twist-rudder (Z), base throttle slider
  • TWCS throttle: throttle axis, analog mini-stick, rocker, plus buttons/hat
  • Best for: X4 & Elite — classic fly-by-throttle feel

T.16000M FCS Space Sim Duo dual stick

Two fully ambidextrous T.16000M sticks.
  • 32 buttons total (16 each), two 8-way hats, weighted bases
  • Ambidextrous: swap the detachable parts + flip the base switch to set one stick left-handed
  • Best for: Star Citizen 6-DoF combat — strafe left, aim right
💡 Match the tool to the game: run the HOTAS (stick + TWCS) for X4 and Elite (throttle-based flight), and the Dual Sticks for Star Citizen (twin-stick dogfighting). Full Elite & Star Citizen binding tables live on the Controls & Bindings page — this page adds the X4 guide and the setup steps for all three.

🛠️ How to set up & play each one

Recommended layout: single T.16000M stick + TWCS throttle. X4 flies great on a HOTAS, but remember it's an empire builder — you'll switch to mouse constantly for the map, trade menus and fleet orders. Bind flight to the stick, leave management on mouse/keyboard.
One-time setup (X4)
  1. Plug in before launching. X4 detects devices at startup — connect the stick & throttle first, then launch.
  2. Turn on joystick input. Options ▸ Controls ▸ Input Method → enable Joystick / Direct Input (X4 supports mouse, keyboard and joystick at the same time).
  3. Assign axes. Under Controls ▸ Space map the axes (see table). X4 lets you set sensitivity, deadzone & invert per axis right there — no external tool needed.
  4. Small deadzones. Hall-effect sticks are precise — start at ~3–5% deadzone and a slight curve for fine aim.
  5. Keep mouse "steering" handy. Many X4 players fly combat on the stick but toggle mouse-steering (default hold Enter / RMB) for docking & menus. Both can coexist.

🕹️ X4-specific tips: bind SETA (time acceleration) to a throttle button — you'll use it constantly for travel. Keep Map one button away since it's your command center. Travel drive (boosted cruise) is the in-system fast-travel — put it on a comfortable base button. The docking computer can auto-land, so you don't need perfect stick control to dock.
⚠️ Reality check: X4 is menu-heavy by design. A HOTAS makes combat & direct flying feel excellent, but you can't run an empire from the stick alone — plan to keep the mouse in reach for the map, trading and fleet logistics. Many players even fly with mouse-steering and use the stick just for dogfights.
