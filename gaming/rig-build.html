⭐ Best match for your dual T16000M: the Marada Flight Joystick HOTAS Chair (~$215) is the shortcut. It's a 300 lb steel gaming chair with adjustable stick/throttle mounts on both arms — the ambidextrous T16000M sticks bolt right onto the left and right arm rests, and the mounts swing out of the way for normal use. No cockpit to build, no drilling. Add a separate triple-monitor stand for screens, and later a wheel + pedals if you want racing. For a plug-and-play space-sim seat, it's hard to beat at the price.

🔧 Mounting the T.16000M

  1. Use the base screw holes. Each T.16000M base has mounting holes underneath — bolt it to a plate or board instead of relying on the rubber feet.
  2. Dual-stick = both sides. Put a side plate at seat-arm height on the left and right; set the left stick to its left-hand ambidextrous config (see Controls guide).
  3. Cheapest mount: a strip of aluminum angle or a plywood board clamped to the frame, sticks screwed on. Cleanest: MonsterTech-style adjustable plates.
  4. Make it swappable. Mount the sticks and the wheel on quick-release plates (or thumb-screws) so you can switch from space to racing in a minute.

💡 Ideas worth stealing from the pro rigs

  • Adjustable side-mount arms (SimFab/MonsterTech) — angle the sticks to your wrists
  • Integrated monitor upright so screens move with the rig — no separate desk
  • Overhead / sub-mount monitor bracket (SimFab) — a screen above for overhead panels & situational awareness, or below for MFD/system displays. Easy to copy with an extra extrusion arm + VESA mount.
  • Fully modular "flight pit" rails (SimFab DCS Edition) — every mount slides on the extrusion T-slot, so sticks, throttle & screens reposition without drilling
  • Seat sliders to change distance for flight vs racing posture
  • Keyboard/mouse tray that swaps in where the wheel deck goes
  • Cable channels along the extrusion to hide the wiring
⭐ My pick for you: a 2040 aluminum-extrusion frame (Tier 2, ~$350–450) gets you the Marada's rigid look, holds both sticks + a wheel, and includes a monitor upright — cheaper than buying the Marada and infinitely tweakable. If you'd rather not cut/assemble extrusion, the Dardoo racing cockpit (~$250) + a $130 triple monitor stand + DIY stick plates is the fastest combo.
⚠️ Reality check: the T.16000M is a joystick — perfect for Elite/Star Citizen, but you can't race with it. A true "space + racing" rig means adding a wheel + pedals and swapping peripherals. Budget for that if racing matters to you.
