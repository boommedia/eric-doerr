🖥️ Triple-Screen Setup

Making three monitors act as one big screen for Elite Dangerous & Star Citizen.
🎮 All Gaming 🎯 Controls & Bindings 🖥️ Triple-Screen 🪑 DIY Sim Rig

✅ Yes — and Elite Dangerous is one of the best games for it.

Elite natively supports triple monitors and ultrawide, rendering a true wraparound cockpit view — the side screens become your side windows. Star Citizen works too (with a stretched HUD). The trick is telling Windows to treat your three screens as one giant display.

Two ways to get "one big screen"

A · Three monitors → Surround

Best immersion
  • Combine 3 monitors into one wide resolution (e.g. 5760×1080) with NVIDIA Surround or AMD Eyefinity
  • The game sees a single ultra-wide screen and wraps the cockpit around you
  • Best with 3 identical monitors (same size/res/refresh)
  • Needs a GPU with 3+ outputs (most modern cards)

B · One big display

Simplest
  • A single ultrawide (34"/49" 21:9 or 32:9) or a big 4K TV
  • No Surround setup, no bezels, way less hassle
  • Less peripheral vision than triples, but plug-and-play
  • Great if you don't already own three matching panels

Setting up triple (Surround / Eyefinity)

  1. Plug all three into the same GPU (not the motherboard). Use DisplayPort where possible; mixing HDMI/DP is fine if resolutions match.
  2. NVIDIA: Control Panel → Configure Surround, PhysX → tick Span displays with Surround → arrange the three in order → Apply. AMD: Adrenalin → Eyefinity → Quick Setup.
  3. Set bezel correction so objects line up across the gaps between monitors (looks like thin black slivers behind the frames — correct, not wrong).
  4. In-game: pick the new wide resolution (e.g. 5760×1080) at fullscreen.
  5. Elite Dangerous: raise the FOV so the side monitors show correct peripheral view (Graphics → Field of View). Community GraphicsConfiguration tweaks exist for precise per-monitor geometry.
  6. Star Citizen: it'll run at the wide res; the HUD/menus stretch toward the outer screens. Totally playable — many pilots keep SC on a single ultrawide and save triples for Elite.
⚙️ Performance reality: triple 1080p ≈ 5760×1080 (~3× the pixels of one screen) and triple 1440p is heavier still. Elite runs it well on a mid-range GPU; Star Citizen is demanding, so expect to drop settings. If your GPU is modest, Option B (one big display) is the smoother path.
