🛰️ The Operations Update

New co-op Operations with lobby & matchmaking, Merc Coin & pre-engineered MercGear, Nomad additions, Vessel Bays and visual overhauls — what's in it and how to use it.
🟠 Career Hub 🛰️ Operations Update 🥾 On-Foot ⌨️ Keybindings
🛰️ What Operations are: a new framework with its own lobby & matchmaking system for running structured, objective-based scenarios. Six Operations ship at launch, mixing ship combat and on-foot boarding actions. Completing them earns Merc Coin, which buys MercGear — pre-engineered modules you'd otherwise grind engineers for.
⚠️ Networking & Balancing Beta: Frontier is rolling Operations out in a beta stage so they can measure server load and tweak things behind the scenes. Expect adjustments, balance changes and occasional instability while it settles.

🚀 How to start an Operation (exact steps)

  1. Dock at any Starport, Outpost, Fleet Carrier or surface port. You must be docked to select an Operation or send invites.
  2. Open the Right-Hand Panel (ship Systems panel) → Home tab → select Operations.
  3. Choose your route: Join a squad (browse open listings), Mercenary Operations (post your own), or Powerplay Operations (undermine a Power).
  4. Pick one Operation + a difficulty — Easy or Hard.
  5. Fill the squad: 1 squad leader + 3 members. Matchmaking fills empty slots automatically, or invite friends/mercenaries manually.
  6. Complete the objectives → earn Merc Coin, credits & materials.
⚠️ Start on Easy. For Hard, the recommendation is a squad of 3–4 with fully engineered gear — going in short-handed or unengineered will not go well.
🎯 Bring both kits. Boarding Operations (Tactical Takedown, Biohazard Takedown) need an on-foot suit & weapons as well as a combat ship — and your On Foot binds must actually be set. See the control map and the Field Manual.
🎯 Ship + suit both matter. Boarding Operations (Tactical Takedown, Biohazard Takedown) need on-foot combat gear — see the On-Foot Operations guide for suits, weapons & tactics.

🎯 The six Operations

Descriptions are from the official update notes. The "bring" lines are prep suggestions based on each objective.
⚔️ Tactical TakedownShip On-foot

Brief: The client's megaship has been compromised by a rival faction. Board the ship and fight through the command deck to obtain intel and trace the origin of the attack — then bring your firepower to bear on the general responsible at their home base.

Two phases: an on-foot boarding fight, then a ship assault on the general's base.

Bring: Dominator suit + laser/kinetic pair for the deck fight; a solid combat ship with shields for phase two.

🔥 Firestorm RescueShip Timed

Brief: Hostages are trapped in a burning station. Break the siege and evacuate those that remain. You must manage the station's heat to buy enough time to finish the rescue, while fighting off hostiles trying to stop you.

Bring: heat management is the core mechanic — pack heat sinks; bring cargo/passenger space for evacuees and enough firepower to break the siege.

🛡️ Reclamation PointShip combat Hard

Brief: The megaship has been hijacked. Eliminate all hostile forces in and around it so the client can recover it. The notes call for your best gear and ship.

Bring: your tankiest combat build — strong shields + boosters, thermal weapons for shields and kinetics for hull, chaff & heat sinks.

☣️ Biohazard TakedownShip On-foot

Brief: A group of researchers is developing a dangerous weapon. Assault the ship, obtain the location and IDs of the researchers, and take them out.

Bring: an assault suit plus intel tools — the Profile Analyser style ID work and door access suggest on-foot infiltration; carry E-Breach and a reliable close-quarters weapon pair.

⏱️ Rapid ResponseShip Timed

Brief: A planetary port is under attack with personnel still trapped inside. You cannot delay — a huge hostile fleet is inbound. If the evacuation isn't completed in time, those inside are lost.

Bring: speed is the objective — a fast ship with good boost, plus capacity for evacuees. Get in, load, and out before the fleet lands.

🚨 Terminal ProsecutionShip combat

Brief: A criminal element has been raiding the system for weeks. Your job is to clear them out.

Bring: a straightforward combat loadout — this is the most bounty-hunting-like Operation. Thermal to strip shields, kinetic to finish.
