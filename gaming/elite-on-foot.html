🎛️ Game modes (quick primer)

Play modes

  • Open Play: shared galaxy with all players — PvP possible
  • Private Group: invite-only server (friends / squadrons)
  • Solo: same galaxy, no other players — safest for learning on foot

In-ship flight modes

  • NAV mode: high speed + Quantum/Frame-Shift travel
  • SCM mode: combat speed, weapons & shields online
  • Analysis vs Combat HUD: blue = scanners/mining, red = weapons
💡 Learning on foot? Switch to Solo or a Private Group so you're not ambushed while you get the hang of settlement layouts & combat.

🚀 Your ship as a dropship — Anaconda vs Python

🛥️ Python friendlier taxi

Medium pad — lands at far more settlements.
  • Fits an SRV Hangar + a decent shield; good all-round on-foot carrier
  • Lands directly at most settlement pads, so you disembark on-site
  • Best default for regular on-foot missions

🛥️ Anaconda large pad only

Huge range & storage — but needs a large pad.
  • Many settlements have no large pad → land nearby & drive an SRV, or take an Apex shuttle
  • Shines when combining on-foot with deep-space exobiology (top jump range)
  • Fit an SRV Hangar + shields against settlement skimmers/turrets

🎯 Mission types — how to play each

Filter by style, then expand a mission for its goal, step-by-step & tips. Pick these up at station mission boards, Frontline Solutions, or as freelance activities.
⚔️ On-Foot Conflict ZoneCombat Dominator

Goal: join a ground war for a faction and earn Combat Bonds + merc rewards.

  1. Go to a Concourse in a system at war and talk to Frontline Solutions; pick a side & intensity (Low/Med/High).
  2. You're dropped at the CZ in a Dominator (bring a laser + kinetic + grenades).
  3. Capture control points (stand in the zone to flip it) and kill enemy soldiers & their reinforcements.
  4. Restock ammo, energy cells & grenades from your team's supply crates and downed enemies.
  5. Win the zone, then hand in Combat Bonds at Frontline Solutions.
🎯 Use cover & corners; shield-disruptor grenade then a kinetic burst melts tough troopers. Bring spare weapons — you can grab dropped ones.
💥 Massacre / Settlement RaidCombat Dominator

Goal: eliminate the personnel or clear a settlement (often illegal — expect a bounty).

  1. Accept from a mission board (or go rogue at any occupied settlement).
  2. Approach quietly; consider disabling alarms first at a security terminal (or EMP them).
  3. Clear the settlement — strip shields with laser/plasma, finish with kinetic; watch for a security dropship if alarms trip.
  4. Loot bodies & containers, then extract before reinforcements pile in.
⚠️ Killing non-hostiles flags you Wanted — clear fines/notoriety at an Interstellar Factors contact afterward.
🎯 AssassinationCombat Dominator / Maverick

Goal: kill one specific target at a settlement.

  1. Travel to the settlement; use the Profile Analyser to identify people & find your target.
  2. Approach — stealth (silenced kinetic / melee) or loud (rush them).
  3. Eliminate the target, then extract before security locks the area down.
🕵️ Cloning a profile lets you walk right up to the target in restricted zones without aggro.
🕵️ Theft / Data HeistStealth Maverick

Goal: steal specific data or goods from a settlement without raising the alarm.

  1. Take a theft/collect mission; travel to the settlement in a Maverick suit.
  2. Profile Analyser → clone an NPC's clearance so restricted doors read you as authorised.
  3. E-Breach or Arc Cutter locked doors & containers; grab the target item + any loose materials/data.
  4. Keep off cameras & avoid alarms; slip out the way you came and extract.
🔇 A Noise Suppressor weapon mod & crouch-walking keep you quiet. If an alarm trips, hit a security terminal to reset it or EMP the panel.
🧰 Retrieve / RecoverStealth Maverick

Goal: find a specific item (canister, data, component) and bring it back.

  1. Head to the marked settlement or POI.
  2. Search containers, lockers & the ground; E-Breach anything locked.
  3. Grab the item and return it to the mission-giver.
♻️ Scavenging / Salvage (freelance)Salvage Maverick

Goal: loot abandoned or damaged settlements for materials & data to sell or fund upgrades.

  1. Find an abandoned / damaged settlement (little to no security).
  2. Loot every container, locker & body; E-Breach / Arc Cutter the locked ones.
  3. Use the Energylink to power up dead consoles & unlock more rooms.
  4. Sell components & data to a station Bartender; keep rare mats for suit/weapon upgrades.
💡 Scavenging is the main way to stockpile the materials you need to engineer your suits & weapons to Grade 5.
⚡ Reactivation / RestoreSupport Maverick

Goal: bring a powered-down settlement back online.

  1. Travel in; there may be scavengers to clear first.
  2. Find the Power Centre and insert/enable the Power Regulator (bring one, or find it on-site).
  3. Use the Energylink to route power to each building's panel until everything's green.
  4. Complete any sub-objectives (reboot consoles, restore comms) and hand in.
🔌 SabotageSupport Maverick

Goal: the opposite of restore — disable a settlement's power or systems.

  1. Sneak in (clone a profile to move freely).
  2. Use the Energylink to drain the power regulator, or plant/enable the sabotage device.
  3. Leave before security responds to the outage.
🌿 Exobiology (freelance)Exobio Artemis

Goal: sample alien flora and sell the genetic data — chill & very lucrative.

  1. Find a planet with biological signals (composition scanner + DSS mapping).
  2. Land, disembark in the Artemis suit with the Genetic Sampler.
  3. Scan 3 samples of each species, spaced the required distance apart (the tool shows the range).
  4. Avoid the wildlife; return and sell at Vista Genomics.
🌌 Pair this with the Anaconda — its jump range gets you to untouched systems where samples (and first-footfall bonuses) are worth the most.

🔎 How to find an abandoned / damaged settlement
  1. Easiest — take a mission. Grab a Salvage, Reactivation or Restore job from a station's mission board; it marks a damaged/abandoned settlement for you and drops a nav target.
  2. Fly a planet that has settlements. Supercruise to the planet, drop to orbital cruise; settlements show as surface POIs. Abandoned/damaged ones are dark — no shields, no security patrols, powered-down consoles (use the Energylink to restore power & open rooms).
  3. Use the System Map. After you DSS-map a planet, its settlements are listed under that body — drop to each and check its status before committing.
  4. Watch for surface signal sources while driving the SRV — many are small salvage POIs with containers & data.
  5. Look them up: Inara, the settlement wiki, and r/EliteDangerous share good scavenge spots & coordinates.
⚠️ There's no perfect "abandoned-only" filter in-game — mission boards are the reliable pointer. Occupied settlements have security & will turn hostile; abandoned/damaged ones are safe to strip.

🥊 On-foot combat tactics

  1. Shields first. Everyone (you & enemies) has a rechargeable shield — strip it with laser/plasma, then kinetic to the body/head.
  2. Use cover & angles. Peek, fire, re-cover; your shield regenerates when you're not being hit.
  3. Manage battery. Sprinting, shields, night-vision & tools all drain your suit battery — carry spare energy cells.
  4. Grenades win fights. Shield-disruptor to crack a tough enemy, EMP to kill alarms & turrets, frag for groups.
  5. Handle alarms. Tripping one summons security + a dropship. Reset alarms at a security terminal, or EMP the panel; clone a profile to avoid tripping them at all.
  6. Watch oxygen. On airless worlds your O2 is limited — refill at consumable points, your ship, or an SRV.
