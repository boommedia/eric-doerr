🔄 Swapping space ↔ racing on the rig

A convertible rig keeps your seat, pedals & screens in place — you only change the front controls. Mount both sets on quick-release / thumb-screw plates and it's a 1–2 minute swap, no tools.

🚀 Flight mode (Elite / Star Citizen): HOTAS side-mounts swung in — right stick + left stick (or TWCS throttle) at armrest height; wheel deck removed or folded down. Optional rudder pedals; many just leave racing pedals on. Load your T16000M control profile in-game.
🏎️ Racing mode (iRacing / Forza / etc.): wheel clamps onto the front deck, shifter on the side rail, racing pedals under foot. Swing the HOTAS arms out of the way. Load your wheel profile in the racing game.
The swap, step by step
  1. Loosen the front deck (thumb-screws or quick-release pins) and lift the wheel off — or fold/swing the HOTAS side-mounts out.
  2. Attach the other set to the same rails: wheel on the front plate for racing, or sticks/throttle on the side plates for flight.
  3. Pedals: simplest is to leave racing pedals on for both; for authentic flight, swap to rudder pedals (optional).
  4. Cables: keep everything on a powered USB hub mounted to the frame so nothing gets unplugged during the swap.
  5. In software: launch the right game and load the matching profile — T16000M for space, wheel for racing.
⏱️ Make it fast: the difference between a 10-minute wrestle and a 1-minute swap is quick-release mounts on both the wheel and the HOTAS. Budget for those (or the MonsterTech-style plates) if you'll switch often. If you swap daily, a rig with a hinged/folding wheel deck is ideal.

🪜 Start cheap, add over time

You don't buy it all at once — each phase is fully playable on its own. Start with what you own, upgrade when you want.

Phase 1 — Play today · $0–$215. Your current PC + your 3 monitors + the T16000M you already own. Desk-clamp or board-mount the sticks, or grab the Marada chair-mount ($215) for instant armrest mounting. → You're flying Elite / Star Citizen tonight. Running total: ~$0–$215

📓 Quick research notes

Convertible cockpit (the shared rig): brands like Next Level Racing (GTtrack / Flight Sim), Trak Racer, and SimFab make frames that hold both a wheel deck and HOTAS side-mounts. Budget clones on Amazon run ~$300–550; premium aluminum rigs $700–1,500+.

Wheel + pedals tiers: entry Thrustmaster T248 (~$300) → mid Logitech G923 (~$400, great value) → enthusiast Fanatec CSL DD (~$700+, direct-drive, what's pictured). All need a wheel deck the convertible frame provides.

Screens: three matching 27" panels wrap around you (best immersion in Elite) via NVIDIA Surround; one 34–49" ultrawide or a 4K TV is simpler. See the triple-screen guide. 3× the pixels = you'll want a solid GPU.
